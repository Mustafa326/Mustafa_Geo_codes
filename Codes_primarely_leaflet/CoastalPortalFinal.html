S.S.T Layers

S.S.T Monthly Day

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) is a measure of the temperature of the water at the ocean's surface. In the context of the Sea Surface Temperature (L3, Day, Monthly, Thermal, 4 km) product from NASA's Global Imagery Browse Services (GIBS), it refers to the temperature of the top layer of the ocean during daylight hours.

S.S.T Monthly Night

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) is a measure of the temperature of the water at the ocean's surface. In the context of the Sea Surface Temperature (L3, Night, Monthly, Thermal, 4 km) product from NASA's Global Imagery Browse Services (GIBS), it refers to the temperature of the top layer of the ocean during moonlight hours.

S.S.T Anomalies

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) anomalies refer to the difference between the observed sea surface temperature at a specific location and time and the long-term average temperature for that same location and time of year. In other words, it indicates how much the sea surface temperature deviates from the expected or typical temperature for a particular region.

Marine Micro-Plastic Emission Concentration (2020-2024)

Layer Description:
This layer provides insights into the extent of plastic pollution across various watersheds and countries worldwide. Utilizing data on mismanaged plastic waste production (MPW) per country, population density, topographic elevation, and the presence of artificial barriers, the layer offers a comprehensive understanding of the sources and distribution of plastic pollution.By combining data on waste generation per capita, population density, and water flow per river catchment area, researchers calculated MPW rates for each area.

COASTAL HAZARD MONITORING 🌊 :- Pakistan's coastal hazard monitoring is a comprehensive initiative aimed at assessing and mitigating potential risks along its coastal areas. With a diverse coastline along the Arabian Sea, stretching over 1,000 kilometers, the country faces various hazards such as cyclones, storm surges, and sea-level rise. The monitoring system integrates advanced technologies, satellite imagery, and on-ground observations to track and analyze coastal dynamics.

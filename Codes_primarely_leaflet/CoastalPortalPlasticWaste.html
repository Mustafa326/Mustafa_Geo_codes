Layer Description:
Plastic waste generation per person, measured in kilograms per person per day. This measures total plastic waste generation prior to management and therefore does not represent the quantity of plastic at risk of polluting waterways, rivers and the ocean environment. The Primary Unit for representations is tones

Marine Micro-Plastic Emission Concentration (2020-2024)

Layer Description:
This layer provides insights into the extent of plastic pollution across various watersheds and countries worldwide. Utilizing data on mismanaged plastic waste production (MPW) per country, population density, topographic elevation, and the presence of artificial barriers, the layer offers a comprehensive understanding of the sources and distribution of plastic pollution.By combining data on waste generation per capita, population density, and water flow per river catchment area, researchers calculated MPW rates for each area.

Coastal Hazard Monitoring 🌊 :- Pakistan's coastal hazard monitoring is a comprehensive initiative aimed at assessing and mitigating potential risks along its coastal areas. With a diverse coastline along the Arabian Sea, stretching over 1,000 kilometers, the country faces various hazards such as cyclones, storm surges, and sea-level rise. The monitoring system integrates advanced technologies, satellite imagery, and on-ground observations to track and analyze coastal dynamics.

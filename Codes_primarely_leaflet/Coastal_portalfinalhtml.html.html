Coastal Flood Projection 50 Years

Layer Description:
The SS-GLOBAL-MUIS-2 dataset presents a significant advancement in the understanding of coastal flood events worldwide. Compiled by Muis et al., this dataset offers the first global reanalysis of storm surges and extreme sea levels based on hydrodynamic modeling techniques.The dataset, known as the Global Tide and Surge Reanalysis (GTSR) dataset, covers the entirety of the world's coastline and provides comprehensive time series data on tides, surges, and estimates of extreme sea levels.

Sea Surface Salinity Monthly

Layer Description:
This product provides information about the salinity of the sea surface on a monthly basis and utilizes data from the Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) mission's radiometer instrument. Salinity in the context of this layer refers to the concentration of salt in the top layer of the ocean. Measuring sea surface salinity is important for understanding variations in salt content, which can influence ocean circulation, water density, and contribute to our knowledge of the Earth's water cycle.

Sea surface Salinity Daily

Layer Description:
This product provides information about the salinity of the sea surface, calculated as an 8-day running mean. It utilizes data from the Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) The 8-day running mean indicates that the dataset presents averaged salinity values over consecutive 8-day periods, helping to smooth out short-term variations and provide a more consistent representation of sea surface salinity trends.

Marine Micro-Plastic Emission Concentration (2020-2024)

Layer Description:
This layer provides insights into the extent of plastic pollution across various watersheds and countries worldwide. Utilizing data on mismanaged plastic waste production (MPW) per country, population density, topographic elevation, and the presence of artificial barriers, the layer offers a comprehensive understanding of the sources and distribution of plastic pollution.By combining data on waste generation per capita, population density, and water flow per river catchment area, researchers calculated MPW rates for each area.

Cyclone Layers

Tropical Cyclone 50 year Return Period

Layer Description:
The tropical cyclone wind map indicates the mean value of wind velocity for a return period of 50 years. The tropical cyclone events used for the elaboration of this map consider climate change based on models representative concentration pathways (RCP) scenarios. The map has global coverage and a resolution of 6' (~11.1 kilometers at the equator).

Tropical Cyclone Population Exposure Per Year

Layer Description:
This dataset includes an estimation of the annual physical exposition to tropical cyclones of Saffir-Simpson category 5 it determines the susceptible coastal zones of cyclones based on various factors

Tropical Cyclones Mortality Risk

Layer Description:
This layer represents a dataset that includes an estimate of the global risk induced by tropical cyclone hazard. Unit is estimated risk index from 1 (low) to 5 (extreme). This product was designed by UNEP/GRID-Geneva for the Global Assessment Report on Risk Reduction (GAR). It was modelled using global data.

COASTAL HAZARD MONITORING :- Pakistan's coastal hazard monitoring is a comprehensive initiative aimed at assessing and mitigating potential risks along its coastal areas. With a diverse coastline along the Arabian Sea, stretching over 1,000 kilometers, the country faces various hazards such as cyclones, storm surges, and sea-level rise. The monitoring system integrates advanced technologies, satellite imagery, and on-ground observations to track and analyze coastal dynamics.

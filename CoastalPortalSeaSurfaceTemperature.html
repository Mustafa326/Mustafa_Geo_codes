Coastal Hazard Monitoring Portal: S.S.T

S.S.T Layers

S.S.T Monthly Day

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) is a measure of the temperature of the water at the ocean's surface.This layer refers to the temperature of the top layer of the ocean during daylight hours.

S.S.T Monthly Night

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) is a measure of the temperature of the water at the ocean's surface.This layer refers to the temperature of the top layer of the ocean during nightlight hours.

S.S.T Anomalies

Layer Description:
Sea Surface Temperature (SST) anomalies refer to the difference between the observed sea surface temperature at a specific location and time and the long-term average temperature for that same location and time of year.

Coastal Hazard Monitoring: S.S.T :- Sea surface temperature, or ocean surface temperature, is the ocean temperature close to the surface. The exact meaning of surface varies in the literature and in practice. It is usually between 1 millimetre and 20 metres below the sea surface. The oceans of the world absorb the overwhelming majority of the heat caused by global warming, creating serious consequences for life in and around them, including humans. The oceans cover 70% of the earth’s surface, and water can absorb tremendous amounts of energy. Oceans absorb more than 90% of the excess heat caused by climate change.Oceans are hitting record high temperatures. The global average sea surface temperature hit an all-time record high of 69.73 degrees Fahrenheit on July 31, according to a data set maintained by Copernicus, the the Earth observation component of the European Union’s Space program, which goes back as far as 1979.Record ocean temperatures cause stronger storms, displace and kill fish populations, stress coral reef populations, turbocharge the growth of harmful algal blooms and in the long-term, cause sea level rise.

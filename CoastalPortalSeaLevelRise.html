Coastal Flood Projection 50 Years

Layer Description:
The SS-GLOBAL-MUIS-2 dataset presents a significant advancement in the understanding of coastal flood events worldwide. Compiled by Muis et al., this dataset offers the first global reanalysis of storm surges and extreme sea levels based on hydrodynamic modeling techniques.The dataset, known as the Global Tide and Surge Reanalysis (GTSR) dataset, covers the entirety of the world's coastline and provides comprehensive time series data on tides, surges, and estimates of extreme sea levels.

Coastal Hazard Monitoring: Sea Water Rise 🌊 :- Sea level rise is an increase in the level of the world's oceans due to the effects of global warming.The rising water level is mostly due to a combination of melt water from glaciers and ice sheets and thermal expansion of seawater as it warms. In 2022, global mean sea level was 101.2 millimeters (4 inches) above 1993 levels, making it the highest annual average in the satellite record (1993-present).Between 1901 and 2018, average global sea level rose by 15–25 cm, an average of 1–2 mm per year. This rate accelerated to 4.62 mm /yr for the decade 2013–2022. Climate change due to human activities is the main cause. Between 1993 and 2018, thermal expansion of water accounted for 42% of sea level rise.

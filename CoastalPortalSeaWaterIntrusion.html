Sea Surface Salinity Monthly

Layer Description:
This product provides information about the salinity of the sea surface on a monthly basis and utilizes data from the Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) mission's radiometer instrument. Salinity in the context of this layer refers to the concentration of salt in the top layer of the ocean. Measuring sea surface salinity is important for understanding variations in salt content, which can influence ocean circulation, water density, and contribute to our knowledge of the Earth's water cycle.

Sea surface Salinity Daily

Layer Description:
This product provides information about the salinity of the sea surface, calculated as an 8-day running mean. It utilizes data from the Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) The 8-day running mean indicates that the dataset presents averaged salinity values over consecutive 8-day periods, helping to smooth out short-term variations and provide a more consistent representation of sea surface salinity trends.

Saline Water Intrusion

Layer Description:
Data Representing the intrusion of the saline water along the coast of pakistan

Coastal Hazard Monitoring: Sea Water Intrusion :- Saltwater intrusion is the movement of saline water into freshwater aquifers, which can lead to groundwater quality degradation, including drinking water sources, and other consequences. Saltwater intrusion can naturally occur in coastal aquifers, owing to the hydraulic connection between groundwater and seawater.As sea levels rise along the coasts, saltwater can move onto the land. Known as saltwater intrusion, this occurs when storm surges or high tides overtop areas low in elevation. It also occurs when saltwater infiltrates freshwater aquifers and raises the groundwater table below the soil surface.Sea Level Rise, Sea Water Intrusion and land subsidence are major emerging issues in several coastal cities of Pakistan.

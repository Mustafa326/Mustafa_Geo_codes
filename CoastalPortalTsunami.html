Coastal Hazard Monitoring: Tsunami 🌊 :- A tsunami is a giant wave or series of waves caused by a huge earthquake or volcanic eruption under the ocean. These occur from movement in the Earth's crust.The earthquake causes a large amount of water to be displaced very quickly. When this happens far out at sea, tsunami waves don't really increase in height. As they travel inland, the speed and height of the waves increases.This can cause a huge amount of damage when the wave of water reaches the shoreline.It’s important to remember that a tsunami is a series of waves and not just one wave, this is called a ‘wave train’. What this means is that you should not assume that a tsunami has passed after one initial wave. You should wait for official confirmation from the authorities that it is safe.

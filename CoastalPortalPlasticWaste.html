Marine Micro-Plastic Emission Concentration (2020-2024)

Layer Description:
This layer provides insights into the extent of plastic pollution across various watersheds and countries worldwide. Utilizing data on mismanaged plastic waste production (MPW) per country, population density, topographic elevation, and the presence of artificial barriers, the layer offers a comprehensive understanding of the sources and distribution of plastic pollution.By combining data on waste generation per capita, population density, and water flow per river catchment area, researchers calculated MPW rates for each area.

Coastal Hazard Monitoring: Plastic Waste :- An estimated 8 million tons of plastic waste enters the oceans every year. By 2050, it is expected that there will be more plastic in the oceans than fish. Plastics pose threat to nature by being non-biodegradable and toxic. A simple plastic bag can take up to 500 years to decompose while a plastic bottle can take around 300 years.As per UN assessments, more than 3.3 million tons of plastic is wasted each year in Pakistan, and if no action is taken, plastic waste is expected to reach 12 million tons annually by 2040, which will lead to significant damage to the environment and human health. This Portal assists in the monitoring of Plastic Waste with respect to the coast of Pakistan
